Quick Glossary — click to expand
RSI

0-100 "tiredness meter". Above 70 = overbought, below 30 = oversold.

RSI Divergence

Price makes new low but RSI makes higher low = sellers weakening = price likely reverses up.

ATR

Average True Range. How much price moves per candle. Used to set stop losses that adapt to volatility.

Pivot Levels

Support/resistance from yesterday's high, low, close. Traders worldwide watch these.

Fib Retracement

After a big move, price often pulls back to 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6% before continuing.

Trailing Stop

A stop loss that follows price up (never down). Locks in profit automatically as the trade works.

DXY

Dollar Index. Moves opposite to 6E. DXY down = Euro up. It's a confirmation seesaw.

VWAP

Volume-Weighted Average Price. The "fair price" for the day. Mean reversion target & partial exit level.

Delta

Ask volume minus bid volume. Positive delta = buyers dominating. Negative = sellers.

Volume Per Trade

Total volume / number of trades. High VPT = institutions (whales) are active at that price.

Exact Rules — Step by Step

  1. Open your 5m 6E chart. Time: 10:00 AM IST.
  2. Look for RSI divergence. Price makes a new low but RSI makes a HIGHER low (bullish). Or price makes a new high but RSI makes a LOWER high (bearish). Any divergence type counts — regular or hidden.
  3. Enter at the close of the divergence candle. No extra confirmation needed. Just the divergence.
  4. Set trailing stop: Initial stop = 1 x ATR below entry (for longs). Trail multiplier = 0.75 x ATR. As price moves in your favor, stop follows but NEVER moves backward.
  5. Force close at 9:00 PM IST if trailing stop hasn't hit yet. No overnight holds.
  6. DO NOT TRADE before 10 AM or after 9 PM IST.

Exact Rules — Step by Step

  1. Open BOTH 5m and 15m 6E charts. You take signals from either timeframe. Time: 8 AM IST.
  2. Spot RSI divergence on either chart.
  3. Check VWAP: Is price BELOW VWAP? → only take LONG. Is price ABOVE VWAP? → only take SHORT. If price is on the wrong side, SKIP the trade.
  4. Wait for the NEXT candle. If it closes GREEN (for longs) or RED (for shorts) = ENTER. If it's the wrong color or a doji, SKIP.
  5. Set trailing stop: 1xATR initial, 0.5xATR trail.
  6. Close by 9 PM IST.
  7. SKIP IF: Price is on the wrong side of VWAP, or next candle doesn't confirm.

Why VWAP alignment works: VWAP is where most volume traded today. If you're below VWAP going long, price is "cheap" vs the day's average — natural pull back toward VWAP. If above VWAP going short, price is "expensive." You're always trading TOWARD the mean.

What NOT to Trade

Red Flags

  • No wick rejection — candles show no fight from the other side
  • Next candle doesn't confirm — if next candle is same color as the trend, momentum hasn't shifted
  • DXY moving same direction — Dollar strengthening while you go long EUR = fighting the tide
  • No price level nearby — divergence at random price, not at pivot/VWAP/fib/session
  • Low volume on divergence bar — no conviction behind the signal
  • During high-impact news — NFP, FOMC, CPI, ECB — avoid 30 min before and after
Your Daily Routine
  1. 8:00 AM IST — Open charts — Load 6E 5m chart + DXY. Check which direction DXY has been moving. Note today's pivot levels, prev day high/low, VWAP.
  2. 8:00 – 2:00 PM — Prime Time (Sniper window) — London-NY overlap. Best liquidity. Watch for Monthly Sniper (Strat F) setups. If LL/HH + DXY confirms, this is the highest conviction window.
  3. 2:00 – 9:00 PM — Daily Driver window — Scan for RSI divergences. Apply Half-Day (A), Everyday (B), or Active (C) depending on your mood and risk appetite.
  4. When you see divergence — Run the checklist: — Divergence? → DXY opposite? → Wick rejection? → Next candle confirms? → At a level? → If 3+ yes = enter.
  5. 9:00 PM IST — Close everything — No overnight holds. If trailing stop hasn't hit, close at market. Take the day's result and walk away.
