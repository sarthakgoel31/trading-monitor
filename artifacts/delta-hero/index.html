The Core Idea

Simple logic: When price reaches an important level (pivot, VPOC, TPOC, session open, prev day high/low, VWAP) AND buyers are stepping in (positive delta + cumulative delta rising) AND price is below VWAP = go long. Reverse for shorts. No RSI, no divergence, no pattern recognition. Just levels + order flow + mean reversion.

What is Delta? (click to expand)

Delta = Ask Volume minus Bid Volume on a candle.

Positive delta = more trades hit the ask (market buy orders) = buyers are aggressive.

Negative delta = more trades hit the bid (market sell orders) = sellers are aggressive.

Cumulative Delta = running sum of delta through the day. If it's rising, overall buying pressure is increasing. If falling, selling pressure.

Delta Shift = when cum delta CHANGES direction. That's the signal. Sellers were in control, now buyers are stepping in = potential reversal.

Why this works at levels: Big players (institutions) place their orders at key levels. When price reaches a pivot and you see delta shift positive, it means institutions are BUYING there. You ride their order flow.

Exact Rules — Step by Step

  1. Open your 5m 6E chart with these studies: VWAP, ATR(14), and Delta (or Cumulative Delta). Also note today's pivots, prev day high/low, session opens.
  2. Wait for price to reach a key level. Any of: Standard Pivot (PP, S1-S3, R1-R3), Fib Pivot, Prev Day VPOC/TPOC, Weekly/Monthly VPOC, Session VPOC/TPOC (Asia, London, NY), Prev Day High/Low, Weekly/Monthly High/Low, Session Open (3:30 AM / 12:45 AM IST), VWAP itself. Price must be within 0.5 x ATR of the level. Levels with 2+ confluences (e.g., VPOC + Pivot at same price) = strongest.
  3. Check Delta: Is delta POSITIVE on the current bar? AND is cumulative delta RISING (higher than 5 bars ago)? = Buyers are stepping in = bullish signal. (Reverse for bearish: negative delta + cum delta falling.)
  4. Check VWAP side: For LONG: price must be BELOW VWAP (buying cheap). For SHORT: price must be ABOVE VWAP (selling expensive). Exception: if at a strong support level (S1, S2, pd_low), you can go long even above VWAP.
  5. Enter immediately. No need to wait for next candle. Delta at a level IS the confirmation.
  6. Set trailing stop: 1 x ATR initial, 0.5 x ATR trail.
  7. Close by 9 PM IST. No overnight.

Why no "next candle" confirmation? With RSI divergence, you need the next candle because divergence alone doesn't mean the reversal has started. But delta at a level IS the reversal starting — you're seeing real buy/sell orders hitting the market at a known level. The institution already placed the order. You enter WITH them, not after.

What NOT to Trade

Red Flags

  • X No key level — delta positive at a random price means nothing. Must be at a known level.
  • X Weak delta — delta of +3 or -5 is noise. Look for delta > 20-30 for conviction.
  • X Wrong VWAP side — buying above VWAP or selling below VWAP = fighting the mean. Exception: strong support/resistance.
  • X Cum delta not confirming — delta is positive on this bar but cum delta is still falling = the overall trend is still against you.
  • X After 9 PM IST — WR drops from 80% to 42% after 9 PM. Do not trade.
Sierra Chart Setup

What to add to your 5m 6E chart

  1. VWAP — Study > VWAP. The blue line on your chart. This tells you "fair price."
  2. ATR (14) — Study > ATR, period 14. Shows as a number. Used for your stop loss distance.
  3. Cumulative Delta Bars — Study > Cumulative Delta Bars (or Delta Volume). This shows delta per bar as green/red bars. Green = buyers, red = sellers.
  4. Daily Pivots — Study > Pivot Points. Set to "Standard" and "Daily." Shows PP, S1-S3, R1-R3 as horizontal lines.
  5. Previous Day High/Low — Study > Previous Day High Low. Two horizontal lines.
