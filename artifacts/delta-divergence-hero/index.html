The Core Idea

The insight: A RED candle at a key level with POSITIVE delta means institutions are BUYING while retail sees red and panics. The candle says "selling!" but the order flow says "we're accumulating here." That's delta divergence. When it happens at a pivot/VPOC/session level below VWAP = go long. GREEN candle + negative delta above VWAP at resistance = go short.

What is Delta Divergence? (click to expand)

Delta = Ask Volume minus Bid Volume on a candle. Positive = buyers, Negative = sellers.

Normal candle: GREEN candle + positive delta = buyers pushed price up with buying pressure. Nothing special.

Delta DIVERGENCE:

Bullish absorption: RED candle + POSITIVE delta. Price went down (candle is red), but MORE buyers than sellers (delta is positive). This means institutions are quietly buying while the candle looks bearish. They're accumulating at this level.

Bearish absorption: GREEN candle + NEGATIVE delta. Price went up, but more sellers than buyers. Institutions are distributing / selling into the rally.

Next candle confirmation: After the divergence candle, the next candle should be GREEN with POSITIVE delta (for longs) or RED with NEGATIVE delta (for shorts). Color + delta aligned = momentum has shifted.

Why at levels: Institutions don't buy randomly. They buy at known levels (pivots, VPOC, support). Delta divergence AT a level = "smart money is stepping in here."

Exact Rules — Step by Step

  1. Open your 5m 6E chart with: VWAP, ATR(14), Delta Bars (or Cumulative Delta Bars). Mark today's pivots, VPOC, prev day H/L.
  2. Wait for price to reach a STRONG level (2+ references). Examples: Pivot PP + prev day VPOC at the same price. Or S1 + weekly TPOC. The more levels stacked together, the stronger. Single level = weaker signal.
  3. Check for DELTA DIVERGENCE:
    • Bullish: Candle is RED but delta is POSITIVE. Sellers pushed price down, but buyers absorbed more volume. The candle lies.
    • Bearish: Candle is GREEN but delta is NEGATIVE. Buyers pushed price up, but sellers dumped more volume.
    If candle and delta agree (both green or both red) = NOT a divergence = skip.
  4. Check VWAP: LONG only if price is BELOW VWAP. SHORT only if ABOVE VWAP. Wrong side = SKIP.
  5. Enter at the close of the divergence candle. No need to wait for next candle — the divergence IS the signal.
  6. Set trailing stop: 1 x ATR initial, 0.5 x ATR trail.
  7. Close by 9 PM IST.

Exact Rules — Step by Step

  1. Spot delta divergence at a strong level (same as S1): RED candle + positive delta, or GREEN candle + negative delta. At 2+ confluent levels. VWAP aligned.
  2. DO NOT ENTER YET. Wait for the next 5-minute candle to close.
  3. Check the next candle:
    • For LONG: next candle must be GREEN AND its delta must be POSITIVE. Color + delta both say "buying."
    • For SHORT: next candle must be RED AND its delta must be NEGATIVE. Color + delta both say "selling."
    If next candle color or delta disagrees = SKIP. Momentum hasn't confirmed.
  4. Enter at the close of the confirmation candle.
  5. Trailing stop: 1xATR initial, 0.5xATR trail.
  6. Close by 9 PM IST.

S1 vs S2 tradeoff: S1 enters immediately on the divergence candle = more trades (3.8/day), higher WR (70%). S2 waits for confirmation = fewer trades (1.2/day), lower WR (56%) but winners are significantly bigger than losers (PF 2.60). S1 is bread and butter. S2 is for days when you want to be selective.

What NOT to Trade

Red Flags

  • X No key level — delta positive at a random price means nothing. Must be at a known level.
  • X Weak delta — delta of +3 or -5 is noise. Look for delta > 20-30 for conviction.
  • X Wrong VWAP side — buying above VWAP or selling below VWAP = fighting the mean. Exception: strong support/resistance.
  • X Cum delta not confirming — delta is positive on this bar but cum delta is still falling = the overall trend is still against you.
  • X After 9 PM IST — WR drops from 80% to 42% after 9 PM. Do not trade.
Sierra Chart Setup

What to add to your 5m 6E chart

  1. VWAP — Study > VWAP. The blue line on your chart. This tells you "fair price."
  2. ATR (14) — Study > ATR, period 14. Shows as a number. Used for your stop loss distance.
  3. Cumulative Delta Bars — Study > Cumulative Delta Bars (or Delta Volume). This shows delta per bar as green/red bars. Green = buyers, red = sellers.
  4. Daily Pivots — Study > Pivot Points. Set to "Standard" and "Daily." Shows PP, S1-S3, R1-R3 as horizontal lines.
  5. Previous Day High/Low — Study > Previous Day High Low. Two horizontal lines.
